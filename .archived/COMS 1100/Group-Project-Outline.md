# Cultural Appropriation within Media and Film: Outline

By Muftah Farah, Luke Mugler, Evan Matheny, Aidan Mckenna, Jay Souza, and Rev Guarino

## Outline

- Defining Our Area of Study: Culture and Media and Film
  - Definition of the term Cultural Appropriation from Cambridge
    - "The act of taking or using things from a culture that is not your own, especially without showing that you understand or respect that culture" (Cambridge Dictionary)
  - Area Focus
    - Visual Storytelling via Media and Film.
    - Cultural Perception influences the way audiences value different groups.
- Bring It On  
  - White suburban cheer team steal a routine from Black and Latinx cheer team.
  - Serves as an example of White institutions profiteering via Minority ingenuity.
  - The routine is copied and then progressively deterioted by the white school.
  - The ethnicity of the cast is similar to the black hats vs swhite hate.
  - The White team offers money to attend competition, demonstrating the power of White privelege.
  - White team is praised for talent, despite stealing. Black + Latinx team is depicted as unsuitables.
- Aladdin
  - Reinforces idea of Arabic and Middle Eastern cultures being sandstorms of violence.
  - Good characters have lighter skin, Evil characters have darker skin.
  - Character designs are a bad mashup of several contrasting influences/cultures.
  - By trying to make an imaginary Middle East, it erases a lot of the Middle East.
- Live Action Aladdin
  - Reinforces a lot of the topics mentiond in Aladdin but now with brownface and even more aesthetic confusion.
- Pocahontas
  - Native Americans are depicted as either mystical and one with nature or savage and ruthless barbarians.
  - Native American women are depicted as monolithically beautiful which feels like its in poor taste considering Pocahontas was forced to be in a relationship.
  - John Smith was many things, none of which was a hero.
- "My Band" by D12
  - D12 often produced satirical, humorous, or shocking music.
  - Plot of the music video is Eminem is chosen as the band's frontrunner by executives and begins to ignore his band.
  - Multiple people during outro use what is intended to be a Mexican/Spanish voice while wearing Mariachi.
- Peter Pan
  - At almost no point in the film were the Native Americans ever referred to by the term "Native Americans".
  - The Native Americans were depicted as unintelligent and their languages were mocked openly.
  - Every single Native American shown had a drum, and pipe-smoking.
  - White men are shown as powerful and competent over 'the red man'.
  - Similar to Pocahontas, Native American women are fetishized to make certain behaviors acceptable.
  - "What Makes the Red Man Red" is a song that fully replaces an origin myth of Native Americans.
- How does Cultural Appropriation negatively affect the Media world?
  - Distorts Authentic Representation: 'Why isn't XYZ person doing XZY?'
  - Full on minimizes, removes, or shows in completion any actual aspects fo the derivative.
  - Reinforcing White Supremacy
  - Degrades the richness in a culture by showing caricature-like views of them.
